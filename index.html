AM4 Socket History

The AM4 socket, developed by AMD, is a processor socket for microprocessors built on the Zen architecture (Ryzen brand) and subsequent. Introduced in 2016. The socket is PGA type and has 1331 contacts. AM4 became the first AMD socket for motherboards with support for the DDR4 memory standard and a single socket for both high-performance processors without an integrated graphics core (similar to Socket AM3+), as well as for budget processors and APUs (previously used different sockets of the AM / FM series). Development of AM4 began in 2015, after the release of the first-generation Ryzen processors. AMD aimed to create a socket that would support both current and future Ryzen processors, as well as processors from other manufacturers built on the Zen architecture. AM4 was officially introduced in 2016 along with the release of the second-generation Ryzen processors. The socket received positive reviews from critics and users. It was noted for its flexibility, upgradability, and support for new technologies such as DDR4 and PCIe 3.0. In 2017, AMD released the third-generation Ryzen processors, which also support AM4. In 2018, the company released the fourth-generation Ryzen processors, which also support AM4. In 2020, AMD released the fifth-generation Ryzen processors, which also support AM4. In 2022, the company released the sixth-generation Ryzen processors, which also support AM4. AM4 is one of the most popular processor sockets on the market. It is used in a wide range of motherboards from various manufacturers.

Key features of AM4:

  • Support for first-, second-, third-, fourth-, fifth-, and sixth-generation Ryzen processors.
  • Support for DDR4 memory.
  • Support for PCIe 3.0.
  • 1331 contacts.

The AM4 socket has supported four generations of Ryzen processors:

Generation Series Architecture Process Cores Threads
1st 1000 Series Zen 14 nm 4-8 8-16
2nd 2000 Series Zen+ 12 nm 4-8 8-16
3rd 3000 Series Zen 2 7 nm 4-16 8-32
4th 5000 Series Zen 3 7 nm 6-16 12-32
